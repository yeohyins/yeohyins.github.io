GAS GIANT

JUPITER

The largest planet in our Solar System

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its powerful storms, including the Great Red Spot, a storm larger than Earth that has raged for centuries. As a gas giant, Jupiter is made mostly of hydrogen and helium, with no solid surface. It has a strong magnetic field and dozens of moons orbiting it.

Surface
Though Jupiter doesn’t have a solid surface like Earth or Mars, its atmosphere gradually transitions into a dense fluid of hydrogen and helium as you go deeper. At its core, Jupiter may have a solid, rocky interior surrounded by a layer of metallic hydrogen.

The Great Red Spot
Jupiter’s most famous feature is the Great Red Spot, a massive storm that is at least twice the size of Earth. This storm has been ongoing for over 300 years, with winds that can reach 430 km/h.

Unique Features
Jupiter has a powerful magnetic field, 20,000 times stronger than Earth's, which traps radiation belts that are hazardous to spacecraft. Its diverse system of moons, some with subsurface oceans, makes it a focus for future exploration.
