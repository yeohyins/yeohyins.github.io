TECHNOLOGY

Rockets & Launch Vehicles

Modern rockets, like SpaceX's Falcon 9 and NASA's Space Launch System (SLS), are designed to transport payloads, satellites, and astronauts into orbit and beyond. These rockets utilize advanced propulsion systems and, in some cases, are reusable, significantly reducing costs.

SATELLITES AND COMMUNICATION SYSTEMS

Satellites are vital for Earth observation, weather forecasting, GPS navigation, and global communications. They utilize high-speed communication systems to relay data back to Earth, enabling real-time monitoring of space and terrestrial activities.

Space Telescope

Space telescopes like the Hubble Space Telescope and the James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) allow us to observe distant stars, galaxies, and exoplanets. These telescopes are equipped with powerful optics and infrared sensors that can capture high-resolution images and analyze cosmic phenomena beyond the visible spectrum.

Space Suits & EVA Technologies

Space suits are advanced wearable technology that protect astronauts during extravehicular activities (EVA). These suits provide life support, radiation shielding, and mobility in the vacuum of space.
